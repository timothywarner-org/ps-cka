How cka-up.ps1 Works

Two-line PowerShell wrapper around vagrant up --no-provision. The interesting behavior is what happens when VMs are missing — --no-provision silently falls through to a full provision because Vagrant can't skip provisioning on a VM that doesn't exist yet.

Key insight: --no-provision only saves time when VMs already exist. On missing or half-built VMs, Vagrant ignores the flag and runs the full provisioner anyway. That's why the first cka-up after a vagrant destroy takes 10-15 minutes — it's secretly a full vagrant up with provisioning, not a warm boot.

What the script actually contains

# cka-up.ps1 — Boot all CKA lab VMs (no re-provisioning)
#Requires -RunAsAdministrator

Write-Host "`n=== Starting CKA lab VMs ===" -ForegroundColor Cyan
vagrant up --no-provision
Write-Host ""
.\cka-info.ps1

When to use it

  • Every morning — VMs are Off from cka-down.ps1 the night before; cka-up brings them back in ~30 sec
  • After Hyper-V Manager → Save State — same warm-boot use case
  • NOT after vagrant destroy — you'll think you're warm-booting and watch the screen for 15 minutes wondering what's wrong
