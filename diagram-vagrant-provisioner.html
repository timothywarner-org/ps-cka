What vagrant up Actually Does

The Vagrantfile defines three Ubuntu 22.04 VMs and runs them through a three-stage provisioner pipeline. Stage 3 (cka-prereqs) is the big one — nine named >>> steps that map directly to the install commands on deck slides 7, 12, 13, and 15.

Key insight: Every command on deck slides 7, 12, 13, and 15 corresponds to one of the >>> steps inside the cka-prereqs provisioner. Slide 7 = steps 2 + 3 (kernel modules + sysctl). Slide 12 = steps 4 + 5 (containerd + cgroup driver). Slide 13 = step 6 (crictl). Slide 15 = steps 7 + 8 (apt repo + install + hold). That's the contract between the lecture and the lab — Vagrant runs them at boot; the demo verifies they landed.

Why this design

  • Provisioners are idempotent. Every step detects "already done" and no-ops on re-run. vagrant provision <vm> can be run any time without breaking state.
  • Each step is logged. /var/log/cka-provision.log on each VM is a step-by-step record with timestamps. If SSH drops mid-provision (a known Hyper-V + br_netfilter issue), you can SSH back in and read the log to see how far the script got on the guest.
  • The two red steps are the high-stakes ones. Step 5 (SystemdCgroup = true) is the single most common cause of kubeadm init failures in the wild. Step 8 (apt-mark hold) is what prevents a future apt upgrade from silently breaking the cluster.
